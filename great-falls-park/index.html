Welcome to Great Falls Park (Virginia)

Discover Nature’s Majesty at Great Falls Park

Nestled along the Potomac River, Great Falls Park offers a breathtaking landscape filled with stunning waterfalls, scenic trails, and abundant wildlife. Whether you're here to hike, enjoy a peaceful picnic, or explore the historic remnants of the canal system, Great Falls has something for everyone. Located just outside Washington, D.C., it’s a perfect getaway into nature, blending rich history with spectacular outdoor experiences

Park History

Rich in History

Great Falls Park has been a vital part of the area's history for centuries. The park is home to the remains of the Chesapeake and Ohio Canal, a key transportation route built in the 1800s. The park was established to preserve the beauty and history of the area and became a National Park in 1966. You can explore the historical Lockhouse 10 or learn about the canal's role in shaping the region at the park's Visitor Center.

Why Visit Great Falls Park?

Stunning Waterfalls:

Great Falls Park is home to one of the most breathtaking natural features in the region – its stunning waterfalls. The Potomac River cascades dramatically over rocks, creating powerful falls that can be enjoyed from several scenic viewpoints. Whether you're hiking along the trails or simply taking in the view from one of the park's many overlooks, the waterfalls are sure to leave a lasting impression.

Scenic Hiking Trails:

The park offers a variety of hiking trails suitable for all levels of hikers. Whether you're looking for a short, easy stroll or a more challenging hike, you'll find a trail that fits your needs. Explore trails that wind along the river, offering spectacular views of the falls, or venture into the woodlands to enjoy the peaceful, natural beauty of the park.

Wildlife Watching:

Great Falls Park is a haven for nature lovers and wildlife enthusiasts. The park is home to a wide variety of species, including birds, deer, and other wildlife. Whether you're birdwatching from the overlook or strolling through the wooded trails, you'll have the opportunity to spot a variety of wildlife in their natural habitat.

Historic Sites:

Rich in history, Great Falls Park is home to historic sites such as the remains of the Chesapeake and Ohio Canal, which played a significant role in the region’s history. Visit Lockhouse 10 to learn about the canal's importance in transportation, or explore the remnants of the canal’s lock system that helped bring goods and commerce to the area in the 19th century.
